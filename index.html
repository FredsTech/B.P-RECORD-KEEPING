GENERAL TERMS

Your systolic blood pressure(sys) is simply the force of blood against your artery walls while your ventricles(the lower two chambers of your heart)contract pushing blood out to the rest of your body.

Your diastolic blood pressure(dia) measures the force of blood against your artery walls as your heart relaxes and the ventricles are allowed to refill with blood.

DIRECTIONS OF USE
  • Take three measurements of your blood pressure using standard equipment recommended by WHO at three diferent times of the day i.e morning,afternoon and in the evening
  • Try to relax and try to stay still for accurate measurements.
  • Input the values obtained into the right fields
